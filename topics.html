First Robotics Competition (FRC)

What is First Robotics Competition (FRC)?

The FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) is an international competition where teams design and build industrial-sized robots within 6 weeks to participate in an engineering challenge. Over 3500 teams compete with robots in their local and international community at competitions around the world, predominantly in Ontario and the US.There are 91 000 members from over 90 countries, and 70 000 spectators invested in the FRC Championships. Participants priceless hands-on experiences in the world of engineering, problem-solving and business. Through FRC, they develop key skills and competences that are crucial in our constantly evolving technological society.


Team 4308: Absolute Robotics.

Absolute Robotics is a robotics team based at the Woodlands Secondary School in Mississauga, Ontario. We are made up of over 60 students, teachers, and mentors who are passionate about entrepreneurship, business, engineering, and technology!

Our Vision.

Absolute Robotics strives to promote STEM within the community by creating a positive learning environment where students can pursue their passion for STEM and develop their skills in engineering, programming, design, and business. Artificial Intelligence

What is artificial intelligence?

Artificial intelligence is the development of computer systems and programs to do natural human processes. We can use artificial intelligence to develop many applications that can affect many different industries including security, agriculture, finance, law, and more.

During the computer tech class, students investigated into what artificial intelligence is and how it can be used to solve problems. Artificial intelligence is a technology that will be greatly used in the future, and students were able to explore this technology through the computer technology class.
